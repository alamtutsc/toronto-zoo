🐍 Conservation of the Snakes 🐍

Snakes have a bad reputation for being monstrous killers. While some snakes are venomous, they use their venom mainly to catch their food (like rats and lizards) rather than defend against us.
In fact, it's been over 50 years since the last recorded fatal rattlesnake bite in Canada (1). Most snake species in Ontario are completely non-venomous!
But that hasn’t stopped people from almost eradicating some species of snakes in Ontario, including the Massasauga Rattlesnake - which is currently listed as Threatened! This means there aren’t many left in the wild.
So, what conservation efforts are being taken to protect the snakes? How can you help? Take a listen to these audio clips to find out!
